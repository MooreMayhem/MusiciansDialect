The inspiration behind Prolific Miz's Let It Go

"I do not mean to be too abstract but one of my favourite writers, Napoleon Hill, once articulated a concept called sex transmutation. Basically this is the idea that men use the energy they get from being around the opposite sex as inspiration to do creative things. I think that is what happened in the year I created the best music of my career so far

So the premise might seem a bit weird but let me clarify. In the year 2012, I was involved in a very passionate relationship with someone who shall not be mentioned, but this person inspired a song called Fuck The Industry. The title later became a theme in the mix-tape I was working on that year. You see fuck the industry was a loud, arrogant and expressive song that gave me a lot of recognition on my hood. It also caught the attention of a lot of producers who were eager to work with a 19 year old boom-bap rap sensation called Prolific Miz.

One specific producer who reached out to me was KaySoul. This guy is a genius neo-soul vocalist, phenomenal producer and dare I say, a close friend of mine. KaySoul reached out to me via BBM, sent me a beat and asked me to spaz on it. I remember smoking a blunt and listening to this beat while falling in love with the patterns. I started writing the very same day and a song called Let It Go was born.

Let It Go is a love letter to the beat but the lyrics(keeping the theme in mind) also explored my frustration with how feeble the artistry in hip hop had become. (So)First you take some old school soul song and sample it, mix it with hardcore boom-bap drums then add my sex transmuted mindset inspired lyrics, dash it with light, echoed singing vocals for the chorus and you have one of my favourite songs"
